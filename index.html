Nelli Vagena

Experimental Nuclear Physics · Radiation Physics · Monte Carlo

Aristotle University of Thessaloniki

I hold a PhD in Nuclear Physics from the Department of Physics at Aristotle University of Thessaloniki, with research activity focused on the experimental and computational study of nuclear reactions, nuclear data evaluation, and applications of nuclear physics in astrophysics, space science, and medical physics.

I have worked as a postdoctoral researcher at the Institute of Nuclear and Particle Physics of NCSR Demokritos, Aristotle University of Thessaloniki, Culham Centre for Fusion Energy (UK), LAPP (France), and CERN (ISOLDE and n-TOF collaboration). I also collaborate with the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA).

My research includes nuclear reaction experiments, cross section evaluation, R-matrix calculations, and Monte Carlo simulations using Geant4.

Research Topics

Nuclear Reactions & Cross Sections

Experimental measurement and evaluation of nuclear reaction cross sections for astrophysical and applied physics.

Nuclear Astrophysics

Study of nuclear processes driving stellar nucleosynthesis and energy generation in stars.

Monte Carlo Simulations

Geant4-based simulations for detector modelling, radiation transport, and nuclear applications.

R-matrix Calculations

Nuclear data evaluation using R-matrix theory for precise reaction rate determinations.

Medical Physics

Application of nuclear physics methods to radiation dosimetry and medical imaging problems.

Space Science

Radiation environment studies and nuclear data applications for space missions and satellite instrumentation.
